## 1. Overview

Briefly describe:
- What this feature / initiative is
- Why it exists
- The single core idea this spec is focused on

> Keep this to 3-5 sentences. Assume execs will only read this section.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Describe the customer problem you are solving.

Include:
- What is broken or missing today
- Why this is painful for customers
- Why now (signals, feedback, strategy, metrics)

Optional:
- 1-2 short customer quotes or themes

---

## 3. Goals & Non-Goals

### Goals
List what success looks like:
- Customer outcomes
- Product outcomes
- Experience principles

### Non-Goals
Explicitly list what this spec does **not** attempt to solve.
This prevents scope creep and misalignment.

---

## 4. Target Customers

Describe the primary customer personas.

For each persona:
- Context / environment
- Key needs
- Pain points relevant to this feature

> Tip: 2-3 personas max. Keep it focused.

---

## 5. Current Experience (Baseline)

Describe how the experience works today:
- Customer flow
- Defaults
- Friction points

Call out:
- Where customers struggle
- What causes drop-off, confusion, or frustration

---

## 6. Proposed Solution

Describe the solution at a **conceptual level**.

Include:
- Core idea
- Customer-visible behavior changes
- Why this solves the problem

Avoid:
- UI pixel detail
- Technical implementation specifics (unless required)

---

## 7. User Experience Principles

List 3-5 principles guiding decisions, e.g.:
- Quiet by default
- Progressive disclosure
- Explicit customer choice
- Respect customer context

These help resolve future debates.
