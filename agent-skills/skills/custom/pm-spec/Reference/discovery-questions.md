# Discovery Question Bank

Use these questions during Phase 2 (Discovery Interview) of the spec writing workflow.
Ask in two batches — never dump all questions at once.

---

## Batch 1: Problem & Audience

Ask these first. Wait for answers before moving to Batch 2.

1. **Who is the primary audience for this feature?**
   - Which customer personas are most affected?
   - Are there internal stakeholders (engineering, design, leadership) who need to be aligned?

2. **What is the core problem you are solving?**
   - What is broken or missing today?
   - Why is this painful for customers?
   - Can you share any customer quotes, feedback themes, or support data?

3. **Why now?**
   - What signals are driving urgency (strategy reviews, leadership direction, competitive pressure, metrics)?
   - Is this tied to a specific planning cycle, OKR, or milestone?

---

## Batch 2: Scope, Timeline & Existing Work

Ask these after the user has answered Batch 1.

4. **What is being built?**
   - Can you describe the solution at a high level (1-2 sentences)?
   - Is this a new feature, an improvement to something existing, or a deprecation/replacement?

5. **What is the target timeline?**
   - Is there a committed ship date or cycle?
   - Are there dependencies on other teams or workstreams?

6. **Are there existing documents or artifacts to reference?**
   - Strategy docs, 1-pagers, prior specs, design mocks, research reports?
   - Meeting notes or decision records?
   - Experiment results or data analysis?

7. **What is explicitly out of scope?**
   - Are there adjacent problems that should not be addressed in this spec?
   - Are there features or changes that leadership has deprioritized?

---

## Optional Follow-Ups

Use these if the initial answers are thin or if the topic is complex.

- **Platform coverage:** Does this apply to all platforms or a subset?
- **Competitive context:** How do competitors handle this today? Is there a benchmark?
- **Success definition:** What would "good" look like 3 months after shipping? What metric would move?
- **Risks:** What could go wrong? Are there known technical constraints or policy concerns?
- **Stakeholder alignment:** Is there disagreement on the approach? Who needs to approve?
